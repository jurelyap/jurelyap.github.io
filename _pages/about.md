**Jurel Yap** is a quantitative social scientist completing his PhD in Human Society Studies (Criminology) at the [School of Criminology and Criminal Justice, Griffith University (Brisbane, Australia)](https://www.griffith.edu.au/arts-education-law/school-criminology-criminal-justice). He believes data should be used for social good — to understand the world better and to solve real problems. That conviction runs through his research, which applies  quantitative methods to questions about community disadvantage, governance, and political behavior, with a particular focus on the Philippines.

Before his PhD, Jurel spent four years as Program Manager and Statistician at the [Ateneo Policy Center](https://www.ateneo.edu/asog/ateneo-policy-center), Ateneo de Manila University, where he led survey design, built original administrative datasets, and contributed to externally funded research totaling over USD 3.4 million. He has, so far, contributed to 19 peer-reviewed, Scopus-indexed publications across political science, criminology and public policy, with an h-index of 11.

His PhD research examines how high local imprisonment rates weaken community social networks and compound disadvantage for young people, increasing crime, victimization, and worsening educational outcomes — using linked administrative data from Queensland and Western Australia.

His core research interests are in the intersection of quantitative methods and social science research, applied to geographical and survey data. He uses his advanced data analysis skills to explore a variety of research areas and has published articles on topics like crime and punishment, political economy, political violence, youth, and misinformation, with a particular focus on the Philippines. Looking ahead, Jurel looks to return home to teach and research by Q4 2026 to Q1 2027. 

Jurel finished his Master of Science (Statistics) from the University of the Philippines Diliman. He earned a double bachelor's degree in Mathematics and Development Studies, with a minor in Economics, from Ateneo de Manila University (Quezon City, Philippines), where he was an active student leader.
